💡 Key Advice from Your GPA

  • Show Your Working: In theory, steps = marks. No naked answers.
  • Master Fundamentals: Ohm’s Law, KCL, KVL are everything.
  • Time Management: 20 min for Section A, rest for theory (15-20 min per question).
  • Know Your Conversions: Balanced Star → Delta = multiply by 3. Delta → Star = divide by 3.
  • Practice: The 50 questions below simulate Section A.
🔍 QUICK VERIFICATION HINTS (How to Check Your Answers)

Kirchhoff’s Laws (KCL/KVL): At any node, ΣI_in = ΣI_out. Around any closed loop, ΣV = 0. If not satisfied, recheck.
Nodal Analysis: After solving, pick any node (except reference) and apply KCL using your node voltages → sum must be near zero.
Mesh Analysis: After finding mesh currents, walk around the outer loop; substitute currents into KVL; if total ≠ 0, you made an error.
Thevenin & Norton: Verify V_Th / R_Th = I_N (short-circuit current).
Superposition: Solve using one source at a time, sum responses, then verify by solving original circuit with another method (mesh/nodal).
Star-Delta: After conversion, measure resistance between any two terminals in both configurations; must match.
Max Power Transfer: Check that P_max = V_Th²/(4R_Th) and V_load = V_Th/2 under max power.
Linearity: If input doubles, output doubles. If not, check again.
Network Topology: Verify B = twigs + links ; independent loops = B – N + 1.
